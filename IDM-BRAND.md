# Industrie du Maroc Magazine — Brand & Editorial Guide

## About IDM

**Industrie du Maroc** (IDM) is Morocco's leading industrial magazine covering manufacturing, energy, infrastructure, mining, agriculture-processing, automotive, aerospace, pharmaceuticals, and technology sectors. Published in French, Arabic, and English.

**Audience:** Industrial executives, government officials, investors, engineers, trade association leaders, and international partners.

**Mission:** Provide authoritative journalism on Morocco's industrial ecosystem — policy, investment, innovation, sustainability, and workforce development.

## Editorial Voice

### Tone Dimensions

| Dimension | Position | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Serious ↔ Funny | Serious | Professional, authoritative; occasional human-interest warmth |
| Formal ↔ Casual | Formal | Executive-level readership; no slang |
| Respectful ↔ Irreverent | Respectful | Critical when warranted, never dismissive |
| Enthusiastic ↔ Matter-of-fact | Matter-of-fact | Let facts and data carry weight; avoid hype |

### Voice Characteristics

- **Authoritative:** We know the industrial sector deeply
- **Precise:** Numbers, dates, names — always verified
- **Contextual:** Every story explains why it matters for Morocco
- **Multilingual:** Content flows naturally in FR/AR/EN with consistent terminology
- **Independent:** Editorial decisions are never influenced by advertisers or partners

### What We Are Not

- Not a trade publication that simply reprints press releases
- Not an advocacy magazine for any political party or ideology
- Not a general business magazine — we focus on industry
- Not a consumer publication — our readers are professionals

## Content Categories

### 1. Editorial Journalism (Core Mission)
- Investigative reporting on industrial policy
- Factory profiles and industrial zone reporting
- Government policy analysis (investment codes, regulations)
- Supply chain and logistics coverage
- Workforce and skills development
- Sustainability and environmental compliance

### 2. Sponsored Content (Clearly Labeled)
- Company-sponsored features (always marked "Contenu Sponsorisé" / "محتوى ممول" / "Sponsored Content")
- Event partnerships
- Industry reports with corporate sponsors

### 3. Advertising (Separate from Editorial)
- Display ads, digital banners
- Classified listings
- Must never appear as editorial content

### 4. Event Promotion
- IDM conferences, awards, summits
- Industry event coverage
- Partnership announcements

### 5. Corporate Communication
- IDM company news
- Team updates
- Partnership announcements

**CRITICAL RULE:** Editorial journalism must never silently convert into promotional copy. Every piece must pass the "Would we publish this if the company didn't advertise with us?" test.

## Trilingual Guidelines

### Language Selection

| Language | Use When |
|----------|----------|
| French | Default for most editorial content; Morocco's primary business language |
| Arabic | For government-facing content, certain sectors, audience preference |
| English | For international investment stories, technology coverage, global partnerships |

### Translation Rules

1. **Never translate company names** — use the official registered name
2. **Never translate government ministry names** — use official trilingual versions
3. **Technical terms** — use the terminology from IDM-TERMINOLOGY.md
4. **Acronyms** — spell out in first reference, use abbreviation thereafter
5. **Dates and numbers** — follow local conventions (French: 13 juillet 2026; Arabic: 13 يوليو 2026; English: July 13, 2026)

## Fact-Checking Standards

### Source Hierarchy

1. **Official government data** (ministries, ONHYM, ANPI, HCP)
2. **Company official statements** (press releases, annual reports)
3. **Industry association data** (AMIF, CCG, FME)
4. **International organizations** (World Bank, IMF, UNIDO, OECD)
5. **Expert interviews** (named, with title and affiliation)
6. **Media reports** (corroborated by at least two independent sources)

### Verification Requirements

- **Production figures:** Must cite source and date
- **Investment amounts:** Must specify currency and whether committed or disbursed
- **Employment numbers:** Must specify date and whether direct/indirect
- **Government policy:** Must cite official gazette or ministry statement
- **Company financials:** Must cite audited statements or official filings

## Headline Conventions

### French Headlines
- Sentence case (not title case)
- Present tense for past events
- No exclamation marks
- Maximum 12 words
- Examples:
  - "Renault Tangier produit sa millionième voiture"
  - "Le Maroc vise 10 GW de solaire d'ici 2030"

### Arabic Headlines
- No punctuation at end
- Active voice preferred
- Maximum 10 words
- Use Modern Standard Arabic (فصحى), not dialect

### English Headlines
- Sentence case
- Present tense for past events
- Maximum 10 words
- Examples:
  - "Morocco's automotive sector reaches new export record"
  - "New phosphate processing plant opens in Safi"

## Article Structures

### News Article (Inverted Pyramid)
1. Lede: Who, what, when, where, why (35 words max)
2. Key facts and figures
3. Context and background
4. Quotes from officials/executives
5. Impact and implications
6. Background information

### Feature Article
1. Hook (anecdote, scene, question)
2. Context setting
3. Main narrative with data points
4. Expert analysis
5. Industry implications
6. Forward-looking conclusion

### Investigative Article
1. Finding or revelation
2. Evidence presentation
3. Official responses
4. Historical context
5. Impact analysis
6. Accountability conclusion

### Data/Analysis Article
1. Key finding
2. Data source and methodology
3. Detailed analysis with charts
4. Expert interpretation
5. Comparison with benchmarks
6. Implications and outlook

## Image and Visual Guidelines

### Photography
- Factory and industrial scenes preferred
- People in professional settings
- Moroccan workers and executives featured
- No stock photos for editorial content
- Captions in trilingual format

### Charts and Graphics
- Use IDM color palette
- Include data source attribution
- Trilingual labels when applicable
- Simple, clear design

### Social Media Images
- Consistent branding across platforms
- Text overlay in appropriate language
- Square format for Instagram, landscape for LinkedIn

## Prohibited Content

1. **Unlabeled sponsored content** — every paid placement must be marked
2. **Unverified claims** — no statistic without source
3. **Disparaging competitors** — factual comparison only
4. **Political endorsements** — analysis, not advocacy
5. **AI-generated images without disclosure** — always label synthetic media
6. **Stock photos in editorial** — use actual facility/people photos
7. **Untranslated ministry/institution names** — use official trilingual versions
