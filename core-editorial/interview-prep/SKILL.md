# IDM Interview Preparation

Preparation is the difference between a good interview and a great one. This skill provides structure for pre-interview research, question frameworks, and documentation for industrial journalism in Morocco.

## When to Use This Skill

- Preparing for an interview with a company executive (CEO, CFO, plant manager)
- Preparing for an interview with a government official (minister, director, regulator)
- Preparing for an interview with an industry expert or analyst
- Preparing for a panel interview or press conference
- Preparing for a phone or video interview

### Step 2: Develop Questions

#### Question Framework for Company Executives

**Opening Questions (Rapport Building):**
1. "Can you tell me about [Company]'s current operations in Morocco?"
2. "What brought you to this position?"
3. "What are you most proud of in the last year?"

**Core Questions (Substance):**
1. "What are your production figures for [period]?"
2. "How has [policy/event] affected your operations?"
3. "What is your investment plan for the next [timeframe]?"
4. "How many employees do you have, and how are you managing the skills shortage?"
5. "What are your main export markets, and how are they changing?"
6. "What challenges are you facing that the public doesn't see?"

**Challenging Questions (Accountability):**
1. "Your competitors claim [X]. How do you respond?"
2. "Environmental groups say [Y]. What's your position?"
3. "The government announced [Z] targets. Are you on track?"
4. "Some analysts predict [negative trend]. What's your view?"

**Forward-Looking Questions:**
1. "Where do you see this sector in 5 years?"
2. "What would help Morocco's industrial competitiveness?"
3. "What is your company's biggest opportunity right now?"

#### Question Framework for Government Officials

**Policy Questions:**
1. "What is the government's strategy for [sector]?"
2. "How will [new regulation] affect industrial investment?"
3. "What incentives are available for [type of investment]?"
4. "How do you measure success in industrial policy?"

**Accountability Questions:**
1. "The original target was [X]. Current status is [Y]. What happened?"
2. "Critics say [concern]. How do you respond?"
3. "What is the timeline for [policy implementation]?"

**Industry Impact Questions:**
1. "How does this policy help/small/medium enterprises?"
2. "What about regional distribution of industrial growth?"
3. "How are you addressing the skills gap?"

#### Question Framework for Industry Experts

**Analysis Questions:**
1. "How do you assess Morocco's industrial performance in [sector]?"
2. "What are the biggest risks you see?"
3. "How does Morocco compare to [competitor country]?"
4. "What would you change about current policy?"

**Context Questions:**
1. "Is this trend unique to Morocco or part of a global pattern?"
2. "What lessons can Morocco learn from other countries?"
3. "How might [global trend] affect Morocco's industry?"

### Step 3: Prepare Your Approach

#### Language Considerations

| Situation | Language Strategy |
|-----------|-------------------|
| French-speaking executive | Interview in French, publish in French |
| Arabic-speaking official | Interview in Arabic, translate for French/English publication |
| English-speaking expert | Interview in English, translate if needed |
| Multilingual panel | Use the language most comfortable for all |

**Translation rules:**
- Always get direct quotes in original language
- Translate quotes, don't paraphrase
- Use official terminology from IDM-TERMINOLOGY.md
- Preserve meaning and tone

#### Recording and Documentation

**Before the interview:**
- [ ] Confirm recording permission
- [ ] Test recording equipment
- [ ] Have backup recording method (phone)
- [ ] Prepare note-taking system
- [ ] Have printed questions

**During the interview:**
- [ ] Start with rapport-building question
- [ ] Record the interview (with permission)
- [ ] Take notes on non-verbal cues
- [ ] Ask follow-up questions on interesting points
- [ ] Confirm spelling of names and numbers

**After the interview:**
- [ ] Transcribe recording (or key segments)
- [ ] Verify names, titles, and numbers
- [ ] Cross-reference claims with other sources
- [ ] Archive recording securely
- [ ] Send thank-you note

### Step 4: During the Interview

#### Active Listening Techniques

1. **Take notes on key phrases** — interviewees often repeat important points
2. **Watch for hesitation** — may indicate sensitive areas
3. **Note non-verbal cues** — body language, tone changes
4. **Ask "How do you know that?"** — for claims that need verification
5. **Ask "Can you be more specific?"** — for vague answers

#### Follow-Up Questions

When you get an interesting answer:
- "Can you give me a specific example?"
- "How do you know that?"
- "What's the source of that figure?"
- "How does that compare to [competitor/time period]?"
- "What would you say to someone who disagrees?"

When you get a vague answer:
- "Could you be more specific?"
- "What exactly does that mean?"
- "Can you quantify that?"
- "How would you explain that to a non-specialist reader?"

When you get a defensive answer:
- "I understand. Can you tell me more about why?"
- "What would you say to people who are concerned about [issue]?"
- "How do you respond to that criticism?"

## Interview Question Templates

### Factory Visit Questions

1. "Can you walk me through the production process?"
2. "What is your current capacity utilization?"
3. "How many shifts do you run?"
4. "What quality standards do you follow?"
5. "How do you ensure worker safety?"
6. "What is your biggest operational challenge?"
7. "How has automation affected your workforce?"
8. "Where do your raw materials come from?"
9. "What are your main export destinations?"
10. "What expansion plans do you have?"

### Investment Announcement Questions

1. "What is the total investment amount?"
2. "How many jobs will be created?"
3. "What is the timeline from announcement to production?"
4. "What government incentives are you receiving?"
5. "Why did you choose Morocco over other locations?"
6. "What is your local sourcing strategy?"
7. "How does this fit into your global strategy?"
8. "What challenges do you anticipate?"
9. "When will you reach full capacity?"
10. "What is your environmental impact assessment?"

### Policy Announcement Questions

1. "What is the specific policy being announced?"
2. "When does it take effect?"
3. "Who is affected?"
4. "What are the measurable targets?"
5. "How will compliance be monitored?"
6. "What support is available for affected companies?"
7. "How was this policy developed?"
8. "What input did industry have?"
9. "How does this compare to previous policy?"
10. "What happens if targets aren't met?"

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Not doing research** — always know who you're interviewing
2. **Asking for basic facts** — don't waste time on things you can find online
3. **Not recording** — always record (with permission) for accuracy
4. **Not verifying** — claims need verification before publication
5. **Being too aggressive** — maintain professional relationship
6. **Not asking follow-ups** — the best questions come from answers
7. **Not noting non-verbal cues** — body language tells a story
8. **Forgetting to confirm spelling** — names, titles, company names
