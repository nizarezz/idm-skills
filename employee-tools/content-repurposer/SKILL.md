# IDM Content Repurposer

Turn one piece of industrial content into many. The best content isn't created from scratch — it's extracted from longer-form pillar content and adapted to each platform.

## When to Use This Skill

- Repurposing a magazine article into social media content
- Creating newsletter excerpts from full articles
- Adapting industrial reports for different audiences
- Building content calendars from existing material
- Maximizing reach of important industrial stories

## Content Atom Types

Every piece of IDM content contains "content atoms" — self-contained moments that work on their own:

| Atom Type | What to Look For | Best Platform |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Key Statistic** | A surprising number or finding | LinkedIn, Twitter/X |
| **Expert Quote** | A bold claim or memorable line | All platforms |
| **Story Arc** | A complete mini-story with setup and resolution | LinkedIn, Newsletter |
| **Data Visualization** | A chart or graph showing trends | LinkedIn, Instagram |
| **Actionable Insight** | A specific takeaway for readers | LinkedIn, Newsletter |
| **Contrarian Take** | A viewpoint that challenges assumptions | Twitter/X, LinkedIn |
| **Before/After** | A comparison showing change over time | All platforms |
| **Process Explanation** | A how-it-works breakdown | LinkedIn, Newsletter |

## Repurposing Framework

### Magazine Article → Multiple Formats

```
[ORIGINAL ARTICLE]
        ↓
┌───────┼───────┬───────┬───────┐
↓       ↓       ↓       ↓       ↓
LinkedIn  Twitter  Newsletter  Infographic  Internal
Post      Thread   Excerpt     Brief        Memo
```

### Output Formats

| Format | Length | Audience | Platform |
|--------|--------|----------|----------|
| LinkedIn Post | 150-300 words | Professionals | LinkedIn |
| Twitter/X Thread | 5-10 tweets | Industry community | Twitter/X |
| Newsletter Excerpt | 200-400 words | Subscribers | Email |
| Instagram Carousel | 8-12 slides | Visual audience | Instagram |
| Internal Memo | 300-500 words | Company staff | Internal |
| Executive Summary | 150-250 words | Decision makers | Email/Print |

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Identify Content Atoms

Read the original article and extract:

```markdown
## Content Atoms from: [Article Title]

### Key Statistics
1. [Statistic 1] — [Source]
2. [Statistic 2] — [Source]
3. [Statistic 3] — [Source]

### Expert Quotes
1. "[Quote 1]" — [Name, Title]
2. "[Quote 2]" — [Name, Title]

### Key Insights
1. [Insight 1]
2. [Insight 2]
3. [Insight 3]

### Story Elements
1. [Human element]
2. [Data element]
3. [Forward-looking element]
```

### Step 2: Select Target Formats

Based on the content atoms and audience:

```markdown
## Repurposing Plan

### Primary Targets (Must Do)
- [ ] LinkedIn post (from key insight)
- [ ] Newsletter excerpt (from main narrative)

### Secondary Targets (If Time Allows)
- [ ] Twitter/X thread (from key statistics)
- [ ] Instagram carousel (from data visualization)

### Optional Targets
- [ ] Executive summary (for leadership)
- [ ] Internal memo (for team awareness)
```

### Step 3: Create Each Format

#### LinkedIn Post

**Structure:**
1. Hook (1-2 sentences, create curiosity)
2. Key insight or finding
3. Supporting data or quote
4. Implication or takeaway
5. Call to action or question

**Template:**
```markdown
[HOOK — surprising statement or question]

[KEY INSIGHT — what readers need to know]

[SUPPORTING DATA — specific number with source]

[IMPLICATION — why this matters for Moroccan industry]

[CTA — what should readers do with this information]

#Morocco #Industry #[Sector] #IndustrieDuMaroc
```

**Example:**
```markdown
Morocco's automotive sector produced 560,000 vehicles in H1 2026. But how many of those parts are actually made in Morocco?

The answer: less than 40%.

This gap between "Made in Morocco" and "Assembled in Morocco" is the sector's biggest challenge — and its biggest opportunity.

Full analysis in our latest issue: [link]

#Morocco #Automotive #Manufacturing #IndustrieDuMaroc
```

#### Twitter/X Thread

**Structure:**
1. Hook tweet (the most surprising finding)
2. Data tweet (key statistic)
3. Context tweet (why this matters)
4. Quote tweet (expert perspective)
5. Implication tweet (what comes next)
6. CTA tweet (read more)

**Template:**
```markdown
Tweet 1: [HOOK — most surprising finding]
🧵

Tweet 2: [KEY STAT — specific number with source]

Tweet 3: [CONTEXT — why this matters]

Tweet 4: [QUOTE — expert perspective]

Tweet 5: [IMPLICATION — what comes next]

Tweet 6: [CTA — link to full article]
```

#### Newsletter Excerpt

**Structure:**
1. Headline (sentence case, under 10 words)
2. Lede (2-3 sentences, most newsworthy)
3. Key data (specific number with source)
4. Expert quote (with attribution)
5. Implication (1-2 sentences)
6. Link to full article

**Template:**
```markdown
### [Headline]

[2-3 sentence lede]

**[Key statistic]** — [Context]

"[Expert quote]" — [Name, Title]

[Implication]

[Read the full article →]
```

#### Instagram Carousel

**Structure (8-12 slides):**
1. Cover slide: Title + hook
2. Problem/challenge slide
3. Key data slide 1
4. Key data slide 2
5. Expert quote slide
6. Comparison slide (before/after)
7. Implication slide
8. Call to action slide

**Design Guidelines:**
- Use IDM brand colors
- Maximum 3 lines per slide
- Bold, sans-serif font
- High contrast for readability
- Include IDM logo on each slide

#### Executive Summary

**Structure (150-250 words):**
1. Key finding (1 sentence)
2. Supporting evidence (2-3 sentences)
3. Implications (2-3 sentences)
4. Recommended actions (2-3 bullet points)

**Template:**
```markdown
## Executive Summary: [Topic]

### Key Finding
[1 sentence summarizing the most important finding]

### Evidence
- [Data point 1 with source]
- [Data point 2 with source]
- [Expert quote with attribution]

### Implications
- [Implication 1 for Moroccan industry]
- [Implication 2 for investors/policy]

### Recommended Actions
- [Action 1]
- [Action 2]
- [Action 3]
```

## Trilingual Repurposing

### When Repurposing Across Languages

1. **Translate key data points** — numbers stay the same, units may change
2. **Translate quotes** — preserve meaning, not literal words
3. **Adapt headlines** — sentence case in all languages
4. **Use terminology from IDM-TERMINOLOGY.md** — consistency across languages

### Language Selection

| Original Language | Primary Repurpose | Secondary Repurpose |
|-------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| French article | French social, newsletter | Arabic/English summaries |
| Arabic article | Arabic social, newsletter | French/English summaries |
| English article | English social, newsletter | French/Arabic summaries |

## Content Calendar Integration

### Weekly Repurposing Schedule

| Day | Activity |
|-----|----------|
| Monday | Repurpose previous week's articles |
| Tuesday | Schedule LinkedIn posts |
| Wednesday | Create newsletter excerpt |
| Thursday | Build Twitter thread |
| Friday | Create Instagram carousel (if visual content available) |

### Batch Processing

1. **Gather source material** (3-5 articles)
2. **Extract content atoms** (15-25 atoms)
3. **Create LinkedIn posts** (5 posts)
4. **Create Twitter threads** (2-3 threads)
5. **Create newsletter excerpt** (1 excerpt)
6. **Schedule distribution** (spread across week)

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing repurposed content:

### Content Accuracy
- [ ] All statistics match original article
- [ ] All quotes are accurate
- [ ] All sources are credited
- [ ] No AI writing patterns (idm-ai-writing-detox)

### Brand Consistency
- [ ] Tone matches IDM brand guide
- [ ] Headlines follow IDM style
- [ ] Terminology matches IDM-TERMINOLOGY.md
- [ ] Company names use official registered names

### Platform Optimization
- [ ] Length appropriate for platform
- [ ] Hashtags relevant and not excessive
- [ ] Links work correctly
- [ ] Visuals meet platform specifications

### Trilingual
- [ ] Key terms consistent across languages
- [ ] Company/government names consistent
- [ ] Data formatted correctly per language
- [ ] Translation preserves meaning
